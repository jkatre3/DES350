Nature is Waiting

Scenic Drive

The scenic drive in Arches passes many outstanding natural features. Drive all of the paved park roads and be sure to visit The Windows Section, Wolfe Ranch and Delicate Arch Viewpoint.

Hiking

Arches contains a variety of hiking trails, many of which are considered easy to moderate. In some cases, trails travel under arches, affording quite a unique perspective on the park's namesake features.

Stargazing

Arches' relative isolation from the artificial light of urban areas makes it an ideal place for viewing the night sky. In 2019 Arches National Park became certified as an official International Dark Sky Park.

Rock Climbing

The rock at Arches offers excellent climbing opportunities, despite its sandy nature. Most climbing routes in the park require advanced techniques.

Camping

The campground at Devils Garden affords phenomenal views. Camp among slickrock outrcoppings at Devils Garden Campground, 18 miles from the park entrance.

A Story in Stone

The story of Arches begins roughly 65 million years ago.

First, geologic forces wrinkled and folded the buried sandstone.

Next, the region rose, climbing from sea level to thousands of feet in elevation.

Today, rain erodes the rock and carries sediment down washes and canyons.

In winter, snowmelt pools, then freezes and expands, breaking off chunks of sandstone.

Little by little, this process turns fractured rock layers into fins, and fins into arches.

Over time, the same forces that created these arches will continue to widen them until they collapse.

While some may fall, most of these arches will stand well beyond our lifetime.
