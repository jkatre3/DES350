10

principles

good design is

one

understanding the past.

In a world that is highly saturated with design, we must be aware of what's already been done. Only then can we extrapolate solutions that are fresh. We can learn from the greats of the past, incorporating things that were successful and discarding those that were not.


two

useful.

Something created solely for visual aesthetics is art, not design. Design tasks present a problem which seeks a solution. It provides something of substance.


three

intentional.

Everything has an explanation. Every move is carefully calculated. We should strive to make connections and logical explanations for all aesthetic choices.


four

subjective.

One particular design will never be everyone's cup of tea. To some, it will please and to others, it will repulse. What's important is that it accomplishes the goal of the project.


five

thought-provoking.

It starts conversations and welcomes questions. Design can spread awareness. It can clearly inform or leave the viewer canvassing things they thought they understood.


six

honest.

It does not try to spread false information. It does not mislead its viewers/users. It is a reflection of the designer's thoughts and values.


seven

long-lasting.

Trends come and go like the swing of a pendulum. Even though trends will likely phase back into popularity, making trendy work stamps a harsh date on the work. Understanding trends can be important to learn the prefernces of the masses, but designers must find a way to add something unique and unexpected.


eight

thorough.

Due to a popular misconception that graphic design is trivial or meaningless, we must be cognizant of the minute. Every font, texture, and composition must have purpose. 70% of graphic design is experimenting with tiny elements that 99% of people will never notice.


nine

environmentally concious.

The earth is our home and she is not okay. While no design is free of processes that may cause the environment harm, it is still important to be mindful of. Every small decision can make a difference.


ten

peer-reviewed.

Rarely ever is a designer creating for themselves. Outside opinions are both neccessary and welcome. More feedback can only lead to better refinement.
